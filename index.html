About Me

Dr. Olalekan Joseph Akintande is a statistician and researcher specializing in Machine Learning, Computational Statistics, and Statistical Learning. With a PhD in Computational Statistics from the University of Ibadan, Nigeria, he currently serves as a Postdoctoral Researcher at DTU Compute, focusing on responsible AI, algorithmic fairness, and data-driven solutions for healthcare and global challenges. Cited over 550 times, his work bridges interdisciplinary applications in energy economics, public health, climate science, and AI ethics.

Research Areas

Research

Machine Learning & AI Fairness

Designing responsible AI for healthcare, addressing label bias and debiasing techniques in medical imaging and predictive models.

Computational Statistics

Statistical learning models for time series, inflation dynamics, and energy demand forecasting in developing economies.

Climate & Energy Economics

Modeling renewable energy transitions, climate indicators, and policy impacts in Africa and Sub-Saharan regions.

Public Health & Pandemics

Optimal control models for COVID-19, misinformation analysis, and psychological impacts using regression approaches.
